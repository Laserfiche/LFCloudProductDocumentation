Creating and Uploading New Documents

You can add electronic documents and photos to Laserfiche with Laserfiche Mobile. You can also apply metadata to these documents.

Android

To create a new document

  1. Browse to the folder you want to create a new document in.
  2. Tap the Add button in the top right and choose one of the following:

    3. Upload Document

    To upload an existing electronic document as a new image document:

    1. Select the application in which the document is currently stored.
    2. Navigate to and select the document.

      3. Note: When uploaded from Google Drive, Excel files will be saved as PDFs.

    Upload Image

    To upload an image existing in your device's photo gallery as a new image document:

    1. Browse your device's gallery for the images you want to upload to Laserfiche.
    2. Tap the circle in the top right corner of the desired images so a green check mark appears inside . Tap the check mark again to clear your selection. All selected images will become part of the same document. You can reorder the pages of the document later.
    3. Optional: Tap the image itself to see it in full screen format. You can select or clear the check mark icons from this view. Tap the back arrow when finished.
    4. Tap the check mark button to create a new document.

    Take Picture

    To upload a picture you take with your device's camera as a new image document:

    1. Tap the bull's-eye button to take a picture.

      2. Tip: A flash icon will appear in the top left corner of the screen (bottom left if you are using your device in landscape mode). Tap it to toggle it to automatic , off , or on . If your device does not have flash capabilities, you will not see the flash options.

    2. Each photo you take will become another page in the document you are creating. For example, if you take three photos, you now have three pages in your new document. If your photo is blurry or oriented wrong, re-take it and you will be able to rotate and delete pages later. The number of photos you take will be displayed in the bottom right (top right if you are using your tablet in landscape mode).
    3. Tap the check mark button to upload the photos, or tap the cancel button to return to your folders without creating a new document.

To name and edit the new document

When uploading an existing electronic document, an existing image, or new picture, you can name it, change its location, generate text from it, and add templates and fields to it.

  1. If uploading an electronic document, you can keep its original name or change it by entering a new name in the name box. If uploading an existing image or new picture, you can delete the "New Document" text and enter a new name.

    Tip: If uploading an existing image or new picture, you can swipe horizontally to see each page in the document.

  2. To save the document some place other than where you navigated initially, tap the folder button . Tap the edit button next to the current location to browse to a new location. When finished, tap OK, then tap the check mark button .
  3. To generate text (from documents that have text, i.e., Word documents):
    • Electronic document: You can generate text from certain types of electronic documents (i.e., PDFs). Tap the Upload Options button . Tap the check mark next to Generate searchable text and tap OK.

      Note: If uploading a PDF, you will have the option to generate Laserfiche pages.

    • Existing image or new picture: Tap the Settings button . Tap the language in which you want to generate text, then tap the check mark button .
  4. If you uploaded images from your device's camera or gallery, the following options are available:
    • Add pages: Tap the Add Pages button . Tap Add Existing Photo to add a photo from your device's gallery. Tap Add New Photo to take a picture with your device to add to the document. See the instructions above on creating new documents.

    • Tip: You can create a document made up of existing and captured photos. For example, tap Add Existing Photo to select an existing photo as one page of a document, then tap the Add Pages button and select Add New Photo. Take a new photo for the second page of the document.

    • Rotate: Tap the image to view the bottom toolbar. Swipe to navigate to the page you want to rotate. Tap the rotate button to rotate the image shown 90 degrees clockwise.
    • Reorder pages: Tap the image to view the bottom toolbar. Swipe to navigate to the page you want to reorder. Tap the two arrows to reorder the pages in the new document. In the Move current page to dialog box, specify which page the image will become. Type a number in the text box or tap the plus and minus buttons. Tap OK.
    • Delete pages: Tap the image to view the bottom toolbar. Swipe to navigate to the page you want to delete. Tap the delete button delete to delete the image currently being shown. To delete the entire new document, tap Done at the top right and then tap the cancel button in the top left.

    • Note: If you are rotating, reordering, or deleting pages, tap Done at the top right to return to the new document

  5. Tap the Metadata button to add templates and fields to the document.
  6. To cancel your changes and return to the document, tap the cancel button .
  7. After making all desired changes to your document, tap the check mark button in the top right to save it.
  8. Depending on your connection speed, uploading an image document may take some time. Tap Recent Uploads in the main menu to see a list of your recently uploaded documents.

To upload a document directly from another app

You can upload documents to Laserfiche from other apps, like iCloud, Dropbox, and Google Drive.

  1. Do one of the following:
    • In the other app, choose to open the document in (or send the document to) the Laserfiche app. Apps will vary in where the "Open in" and "Send to" options are.
    • Tap the Add button in the top right and choose Upload Document. Select the document you want to upload from one of the available storage providers (i.e., iCloud, Google Drive, DropBox).

    2. Note: When uploaded from Google Drive, Excel files will be saved as ZIP files with HTML and CSS files in them. If you open the HTML file, you can view the spreadsheet as a web page.

  2. See the To name and edit the new document section above to finish uploading the document.
