Viewing Electronic Documents

Once you have located an electronic document (e.g., Word, Excel, or PDF), tap it to open it.

The electronic document will open in one of the following ways:

  • If it has a Laserfiche image associated with it, the image will open by default.
  • If it has no image associated with it, the electronic document will open in your device's default application for that type of electronic document or you will be prompted to choose an application to open the document with.
  • If it has no image associated with it, it will open in a read-only state in the app. All Microsoft Office, iWork, and PDF documents will be shown, and the app will also open audio and visual files recognized by QuickTime. Some electronic documents are not compatible to be viewed on the iOS app, e.g., CAD drawing. You must export these documents to view them.
  • PDFs will open in a read-only state in the Laserfiche Mobile application.
  • The document's text will only open when it's the only thing that exists for that document.

Note: If you choose to open an electronic document with another application on your device, you are effectively exporting the document.

If you cannot, or choose not to, open an electronic document from Laserfiche Mobile, you can still edit its field information, email a link to it, add it to a library, and move it or copy it. If using an Android device, long press the document in the Folder Browser to choose one of these options. If using an Apple device, swipe left on the document to choose one of these options.

To open an electronic document in another application

  1. Open the document in the Laserfiche app.
  2. Tap the More button in the top right .
  3. Tap Open In....
  4. If your device does not have a default application that opens the electronic document, select the application you want to open the electronic document with.

To Interact with electronic files in Laserfiche Mobile

All Microsoft Office, iWork, and PDF documents will open directly in the Document Viewer. Audio and visual files recognized by QuickTime will also open here (depending on file size). However, some electronic documents are not compatible to be viewed on the iOS app, e.g., CAD drawing. You must open these documents in another app to view them.

Some electronic files, such as PDFs and Microsoft Office documents will open directly in the Document Viewer.

To zoom in

  • Double-tap, with one finger, on the specific portion of the document you want to zoom in on.
  • Pinch outward to zoom in to a document.

To zoom out

  • Pinch inward to zoom out of a document.

More options

  • Tap the More button in the top right and then tap Switch View to view the text and/or image component of the document (if applicable). You can also email a link, add it to a library, view its properties, or export the electronic document portion of the document (if applicable).
  • Tap the metadata button to edit the document's fields.
  • Tap the annotation button to view annotations on the document.
  • Tap the back button to return to the document's folder.
  • If using an Android device or iPad, tap the close button to close the document.
  • The page numbers in the upper left display which page you are viewing. However, you cannot tap these number to navigate to a specific page like you can in the image or text view.
