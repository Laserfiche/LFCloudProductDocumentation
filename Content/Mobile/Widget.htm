Widget

The Laserfiche Mobile app for Android has a widget that lets you easily upload new images and documents to Laserfiche.

To add the widget to your home page

  • The process of adding a widget to your home screen will vary according to your device. On some devices, widgets are available through the All Apps option. Other devices have an Add to Home Screen option or allow you to long tap the home screen to select widgets.
  • Navigate to the Laserfiche Mobile widget and drag it to your device's screen.

To use the widget

Tap one of the following buttons to add a document to Laserfiche

Note: If you are not logged into Laserfiche, you will be prompted to log in before the new document is created

Electronic document

  1. Select the application in which the document is currently stored.
  2. Navigate to and select the document.
  3. Name and edit the document.

Existing image

  1. Browse your device's gallery for the images you want to upload to Laserfiche.
  2. Tap the check mark icon on the desired images so the icon turns green . Tap the check mark again to clear your selection. All selected images will become part of the same document. You can reorder the images later.
  3. Optional: Tap the image itself to see it in full screen format. You can select or clear the check mark icons from this view. Tap the back arrow when finished.
  4. Tap the check mark button to create a new document.
  5. Name and edit the document.

New photo

Note: When you capture pages using the camera, they are uploaded and saved in Laserfiche, not in your device's photo gallery.

  1. Tap the bull's-eye button to take a picture.
  2. Each photo you take will become another page in the document you are creating. For example, if you take three photos, you now have three pages in your new document. If your photo is blurry or oriented wrong, re-take it and you will be able to rotate and delete pages later. The number of photos you take will be displayed in the bottom right.
  3. Tap the check mark button to upload the photos.
  4. Name and edit the document.
