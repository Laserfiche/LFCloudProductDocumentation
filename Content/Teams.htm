Teams

Teams provide a way to organize and route tasks in Laserfiche Forms. Tasks can be assigned to an entire team and be visible to all team members in a central list. From this list, team members can chose to complete tasks that interest them or managers can directly assign the tasks to team members.

Example: The City of Scranton gets about 50 records requests a day. These requests come from community members who fill out a form on the city's website. The request forms are routed as tasks to the Records Department for processing. Instead of routing all these tasks to a single individual, the tasks go to the team's list of available tasks. (The tasks can also be found in each team member's list of available tasks.) Different team members interact with this list of team tasks in different ways. Sally skims through the lists for tasks that interest her and tasks concerning property rights (her area of specialty). She assigns all those tasks to herself so the rest of the team knows not to work on them. Mark has a hectic schedule, but he'll check the list when he has a few spare minutes and work on any tasks that can be completed quickly. Around mid-day, team manager Jan will go down the list and assign tasks directly to Jim and Lynn the most junior members of the team.

Team management

Team managers can do the following:

  • Edit the team
    • Add, edit, and delete team members
    • Add, edit, and delete team roles
    • Create and remove managers
    • Determine if team members can see all team tasks
  • See assigned team tasks
  • Change the due date and priority of team tasks
  • Reassign tasks to other users or groups

Creating a team

  1. Click the Administration icon in the top right corner.
  2. Select Team Management.
  3. From the Add team drop-down menu, select Create team.
  4. In the Create Team dialog box, enter a name for the team and short description.
  5. The the Task Visibility section, you can select to have all team tasks be visible to all team members. Alternatively, you can select to only show team tasks to managers and to the team members specifically assigned to the tasks. With the latter option, if a team member is not assigned to a task, they will not see that task in the team task list or in their list of available tasks.
  6. Under Members, type the names of users you want to add to the team, and click Add.
  7. By default, the user you are signed in as will be the team's manager. To make another user the manager, use the drop-down menu next to their name to select Manager. The team must always have one manager.
  8. Optional: You can remove users from a team by clicking the X to the right of their name.
  9. Click Create.

Importing or exporting a team

If you want to copy your teams between Forms servers, you can export teams as XML files from one server and import them on another server.

To export a team

  1. Click the Administration icon in the top right corner.
  2. Select Team Management.
  3. Select the team you want to export,
  4. Click the Export button in the top right, and save the XML file.

To import a team

  1. Click the Administration icon in the top right corner.
  2. Select Team Management.
  3. From the Add team drop-down menu, select Import team.
  4. Either drag an exported team file (.xml) into the Import Team dialog box, or click Select file to browse for a team file.

    5. Note: If the team you are importing already exists on the Forms server, the teams will be merged. Any members, managers, and roles not already on the team will be added. These roles will be applied to both new and existing members. Existing members, managers, and roles will not otherwise be affected.

  5. Continue to import teams by clicking Select another file, or click Done.

Editing teams

Accessing team management options

  • Managers can edit a team by clicking the team's name and selecting Team members.

  • Administrators can edit teams by clicking the Administration icon in the top right corner, and selecting Team Management. Then select the team you want to edit.

Editing team members

  • Add team members: In the Members tab, click Add Members.
    1. Under Members, type the names of the users you want to add to the team.
    2. Click Make a manager, to make the new members managers.
    3. Under Roles, select one or more roles for the team member. Click Create role to create a new role directly from this dialog box. Roles can be used in the process modeler to automatically assign tasks to specific users. Learn more.
    4. Click Save.
  • Remove team members: In the Members tab, click the more button next to the team member you want to remove. Select Remove from team.
  • Designate or remove a manager: In the Members tab, click the more button next to the team member you want to make a manager or remove the manager status from. Select Make a manager or Remove manager status. The team must have at least one manager.

Editing roles

  • Create or edit a role: In the Roles tab, either click Create role to make a new role or click the more button next to an existing role and select Edit role. In the dialog box that opens, define the roles name, description, and members. Roles can be used in the process modeler to automatically assign tasks to specific users. Learn more.
  • Assign roles to team members: In the Members tab, click the more button next to the team member you want to edit. Select Assign roles. In the Assign Roles dialog box, add or remove roles as needed. Click Create role to create a new role directly from this dialog box. After assigning all desired roles, click Save.

Editing team information

  • Edit the team name or description: Click the Edit link next to the team's name. In the Edit Team dialog box, modify the name and description as needed.
  • Edit task visibility: The task visibility setting is noted below the team name. Either all team tasks will be visible to all team members, or team tasks will only appear to managers and to the team members specifically assigned to the tasks. With the latter option, if a team member is not assigned to a task, they will not see that task in the team task list or in their list of available tasks. You can change this setting by clicking the Show tasks or Hide tasks link.

Deleting a team

  • Delete the team entirely: Click the Edit link next to the team's name. In the Edit Team dialog box, click the Delete team button.

Completing team tasks

Team tasks are listed in the Tasks section under each team.

  • The tasks in the Assigned tab have been assigned to a single user on the team. (These tasks will also appear in the My Tasks tab for the user they are assigned to.)
  • The tasks in the Available tab can be claimed by team members, or a manager can assign them to specific users. Team members will also see unassigned team tasks in the Available tab when viewing their tasks.

To complete a team task

  1. Click the task to open it.
  2. Click the Assign to me button.
  3. Complete the form, and submit it.
  4. Completed team tasks will appear in the Completed tab.

Assigning team tasks to others or yourself

You can assign tasks to yourself as a way to let your teammates and manager know that you will take responsibility for completing the task.

To assign a task to yourself

  1. Select a task in the Available tab.

    2. Tip: You can search for and categorize tasks to easily find the ones you want to assign. Use the search box at the top of the page to search by the process name, the task name, or the person who submitted the form or started the process.

    Use the drop-down menu to categorize the tasks by date assigned, priority, process, or who started the task. Alternatively, you can sort each column by clicking on the column's name.

  2. Click the Assign to me button.
  3. The task will now appear in your My Tasks list.

Tip: If you later decide you aren't going to complete the task, you can release it back to the original group of users it was assigned to. Click the more menu on the task, and select Release. Alternatively, follow the instructions below to reassign the task to someone else.

To assign a task to someone else

Managers can assign team tasks to specific individuals or to a group of users. Individuals who have been assigned tasks can reassign those specific tasks to someone else or to a group of users.

Note: Users and managers can only assign tasks if the Allow task to be reassigned option is selected in the User Task. If this option is not selected, then users can only assign tasks to themselves but not to anyone else. Learn more.

  1. Navigate to the task you want to reassign.

Changing the due date or priority of team tasks

  1. Navigate to the task you want to change the due date or priority of .
  2. Click the more menu next to the task and select Edit due date or Edit priority.
  3. Configure a new due date or priority and click OK.

Automatically routing tasks to a team or subset of a team

You can configure processes to automatically route specific tasks to a team or to a subset of a team by configuring the Assign tab of the User Task in the Process Modeler.

Reviewing completed tasks

Completed team tasks are listed in the Completed tab.

  1. Click the Completed tab.

    2. Tip: You can search for and categorize tasks to easily find the ones you want to review. Use the search box at the top of the page to search by the process name, the task name, or the person who submitted the form or started the process.

    Use the drop-down menu to categorize the tasks by date completed, process, or who started the task. Alternatively, you can sort each column by clicking on the column's name.

  2. To see even more information, click a task. A pane will open with the task's status, the step it's currently on, its process timeline (the tasks completed in the process), and the form itself. Click X to close the pane.
